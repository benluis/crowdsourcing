## Content for: Methodology.tex

```latex
To find out what effect ChatGPT's launch had on crowdfunding, we use a method called \textbf{Difference-in-Differences (DiD)}. This is a statistical technique that lets us find causal effects from real-world data, much like a controlled experiment. We treat the public launch of ChatGPT on November 30, 2022, as a natural experiment that changed the environment for project creators.

Our study compares two groups, projects on Kickstarter and projects on Indiegogo, before and after this date. The DiD method works by comparing the change in funding on one platform to the change in funding on the other. This helps us filter out other factors, like general economic trends, and isolate the real effect of the ChatGPT launch.

Our main model tests how three things interact: the quality of the project's writing (\texttt{text\_quality}), the crowdfunding platform (\texttt{indiegogo}), and the time period (\texttt{PostGPT}). This lets us ask a very specific question: was the change in the value of good writing different for Kickstarter than it was for Indiegogo?

To make sure our results are reliable, we also included several control variables in our model. These account for a project's funding goal, the length of its description, its category, and the specific month and year it was launched. This method works under one key assumption, known as the \textbf{parallel trends assumption}. This means we assume that if ChatGPT had never been released, the funding trends for Kickstarter and Indiegogo would have continued to move in a similar, parallel fashion.
```

## Content for: Data.tex

```latex
\subsection*{Our Data}
We began with a dataset of \textbf{64,488} crowdfunding projects from Kickstarter and Indiegogo, running from 2020 to 2024. To make a fair comparison, we focused our analysis on a specific subset of these projects. We only included projects that used US Dollars, raised at least some money, and had complete data for our key measures. This left us with a final sample of \textbf{25,375} projects for our main analysis.

For projects on Indiegogo that were missing a funding goal, we calculated the goal using the total money raised and the percentage of the goal that was met. This allowed us to include more projects in our study.

\subsection*{Key Measures}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Project Funding:} The main outcome we measured was the total money pledged to a project. We used the natural log of this number (\texttt{log\_pledged\_amount}) to make our statistical model more reliable.
    \item \textbf{Text Quality:} A score (\texttt{text\_quality}) that measures how well-written a project's description is.
    \item \textbf{AI Score:} A score (\texttt{ai\_score}) from an AI-detection model that estimates the chance that a project's description was written by AI.
    \item \textbf{Time Period:} A simple flag (\texttt{PostGPT}) that is set to 1 for projects launched after November 30, 2022, and 0 for projects launched before.
    \item \textbf{Platform:} A flag (\texttt{indiegogo}) that is set to 1 for Indiegogo projects and 0 for Kickstarter projects.
\end{itemize}
```

## Content for: Results.tex

```latex
Our final model explains about 46\% of the variation in project funding ($R^2 = 0.456$) and shows three main findings.

\subsection*{Finding 1: Before ChatGPT, Good Writing Mattered}
Before ChatGPT was released, better writing had a clear and positive impact on funding for Kickstarter projects. Our model estimates that for every one-point increase in text quality, funding increased significantly (coefficient = +2.41, p = 0.003). At the same time, projects with writing that seemed to be generated by AI were penalized, raising less money (coefficient = -0.20, p = 0.006).

\subsection*{Finding 2: After ChatGPT, the Value of Good Writing Fell Sharply}
This all changed after the launch of ChatGPT. Our model shows that the positive effect of good writing on funding significantly decreased (interaction coefficient = -5.35, p < 0.001). For a typical Kickstarter project, the effect of text quality flipped from being a strong positive to a negative (from +2.41 to -2.94). This suggests that when anyone can generate good text easily, it is no longer a strong signal of a high-quality project. At the same time, the penalty for using AI-like text grew even stronger (interaction coefficient = -0.37, p < 0.001).

\subsection*{Finding 3: The Change Was Similar on Both Platforms}
Finally, we tested if this drop in the value of good writing was different for Indiegogo compared to Kickstarter. The model's key test for this was \textbf{not statistically significant} (p = 0.436). This means that while the value of good writing fell across the board, we do not have enough evidence to say that this change was any different on one platform versus the other. Both Kickstarter and Indiegogo seem to have been affected in a similar way.
```
